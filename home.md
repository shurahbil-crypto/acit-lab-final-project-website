# Home Page – Info-Based Content Tasks

## Goal

Create an informational home page that explains hackathons clearly to beginners.
The page should educate users, build trust, and prepare them for deeper sections of the platform.
No links to upcoming hackathons on this page.

---

## Section 1: Hero Section

### Objective

Introduce the platform and clearly communicate what hackathons are and why they matter.

### Tasks

- Add a clear headline explaining the purpose of the platform
- Add a short subheading describing hackathons in simple language
- Keep tone educational, not promotional
- Avoid calls to action related to registration or schedules

---

## Section 2: What Are Hackathons?

### Objective

Explain hackathons to users with no prior knowledge.

### Tasks

- Define what a hackathon is
- Explain duration and basic structure
- Clarify that hackathons focus on learning and collaboration
- Avoid technical jargon
- Keep content concise and beginner-friendly

---

## Section 3: Why Hackathons Exist

### Objective

Explain the purpose and value of hackathons in the tech ecosystem.

### Tasks

- Explain why hackathons are organized
- Highlight innovation and problem-solving
- Mention learning-by-doing concept
- Explain why companies, universities, and communities host hackathons

---

## Section 4: Benefits of Participating in Hackathons

### Objective

Show how hackathons help participants grow.

### Tasks

- List technical benefits (skills, projects, experience)
- List non-technical benefits (teamwork, communication)
- Emphasize learning over winning
- Keep bullet points scannable

---

## Section 5: Online vs On-Site Hackathons

### Objective

Help users understand different hackathon formats.

### Tasks

- Explain online hackathons
- Explain on-site hackathons
- Compare advantages and limitations
- Avoid suggesting one is better than the other
- Keep comparison neutral and factual

---

## Section 6: Who Can Participate?

### Objective

Remove fear and lower entry barriers for beginners.

### Tasks

- Clarify that beginners are welcome
- Mention different roles (developers, designers, planners)
- Emphasize mixed-skill teams
- Avoid implying expert-level requirements

---

## Section 7: Skills Gained Through Hackathons

### Objective

Explain skill development outcomes clearly.

### Tasks

- Separate technical and soft skills
- Explain real-world relevance of these skills
- Avoid naming too many technologies
- Focus on transferable skills

---

## Section 8: Common Misconceptions About Hackathons

### Objective

Correct false assumptions that stop users from participating.

### Tasks

- Address “hackathons are only for experts”
- Address “you must win to benefit”
- Address “hackathons are only about coding”
- Keep tone reassuring and factual

---

## Section 9: How Hackathons Impact Careers

### Objective

Explain long-term value without exaggeration.

### Tasks

- Explain how hackathons help with portfolios
- Explain recruiter and company perspective
- Mention networking and exposure
- Avoid promises like guaranteed jobs

---

## Section 10: Platform Purpose Statement

### Objective

Explain why this website exists.

### Tasks

- Describe the platform as an educational hub
- Explain focus on awareness and guidance
- Keep wording neutral and informational
- Do not mention schedules or registrations

---

## General Content Rules

- Keep language simple and clear
- Avoid marketing or promotional tone
- No external or internal links
- No dates, events, or schedules
- Focus on education and awareness
- Mobile-friendly paragraph length

---

## Completion Criteria

- All sections written in neutral informational tone
- Content understandable by non-technical users
- No calls to action related to events
- Consistent structure across sections
